    The Feynman Technique

    The Feynman Technique is a learning method developed by physicist Richard Feynman, emphasizing understanding over memorization, by explaining a concept as if teaching it to someone who knows nothing about it.

    Core Principle:

    The technique revolves around the idea that true understanding is achieved when you can explain a concept in simple terms and identify knowledge gaps.

    Four Steps:

    • 1. Choose a Concept: Select a topic or concept you want to learn or understand better.
    • 2. Teach it to a Child: Pretend you're explaining the concept to someone with no prior knowledge, using simple language and avoiding jargon.
    • 3. Identify Knowledge Gaps: If you struggle to explain the concept clearly or encounter difficulties, it indicates a gap in your understanding. Go back to your source material to fill those gaps.
    • 4. Simplify and Refine: Once you've addressed the knowledge gaps, simplify your explanation and use analogies or examples to make the concept easier to grasp and remember.
