    10 Effective Study Techniques

    • 1. Active Recall

      Focus on retrieving information from memory rather than re-reading. After studying a topic, test yourself on the material. This strengthens your memory and reinforces learning.

    • 2. Spaced Repetition

      Review material at increasing intervals over time. Use apps like Anki or Quizlet, or simply review notes periodically to ensure long-term retention.

    • 3. Pomodoro Technique

      Break your study sessions into intervals (usually 25 minutes), followed by a short break (5 minutes). After four sessions, take a longer break (15-30 minutes).

    • 4. Mind Mapping

      Create visual diagrams to connect ideas and concepts. This helps you see the bigger picture and how different topics relate to each other.

    • 5. SQ3R Method

      This stands for Survey, Question, Read, Recite, and Review. Itâ€™s a reading comprehension strategy where you preview the material, ask questions, read actively, summarize, and review.

    • 6. Feynman Technique

      Teach the material to someone else or explain it in simple terms as if you're teaching a child. If you can explain it clearly, you understand it well.

    • 7. Chunking

      Break large amounts of information into smaller, more manageable "chunks." This makes it easier to remember complex ideas.

    • 8. Study in Intervals

      Instead of cramming, study a little bit each day. This helps prevent overload and improves retention over time.

    • 9. Mnemonics

      Use memory aids like acronyms, rhymes, or associations to make complex information easier to remember. For example, "PEMDAS" for the order of operations in math.

    • 10. Create a Study Schedule

      Plan out your study time with specific goals and time blocks. Consistency and organization help prevent procrastination and reduce stress.
