    Spaced Repetition Study Technique

    Spaced repetition is a learning technique that involves reviewing material at increasing intervals to enhance long-term memory retention and combat forgetting. It contrasts with "cramming" by focusing on distributed practice over time, rather than intense, short-term learning.

    How it works:

    Spaced repetition leverages the "forgetting curve," which shows how we naturally lose information over time. By strategically reviewing material before it's forgotten, you reinforce memory and improve long-term retention.

    • Active Recall: Spaced repetition often involves active recall, where you try to remember information from memory rather than passively rereading it.
    • Increasing Intervals: The intervals between reviews are gradually increased, starting with short intervals and then moving to longer ones.

    Examples:

    • Reviewing lecture notes a few hours after class, then again a day later, and then a week later.
    • Using flashcards or apps like Anki to review vocabulary or concepts at increasing intervals.
    • Revisiting study materials on a schedule, such as 2, 3, 5, and 7 days after initial learning.

    Benefits:

    • Improved long-term memory retention.
    • More efficient learning compared to cramming.
    • Reinforcement of learning through repeated recall.

    Spaced Repetition Apps:

    • Anki and Remnote are popular apps for spaced repetition.

    Spaced Repetition Algorithms:

    • There are different algorithms used in spaced repetition systems, including the Leitner system, SM family of algorithms, and DASH family.
